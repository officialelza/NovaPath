Mission Details

Technical specifications, equipment, and methodology behind the ISS signal tracking campaign.

🛰️

International Space Station (ISS)

The ISS operates a cross-band repeater that relays amateur radio signals, enabling ground stations to communicate through the station as it passes overhead. The signal was tracked on the UHF downlink band.

Frequency Range
437.790 – 437.810 MHz
Signal Type
Sinusoidal Wave
Mode
Cross Repeater
Tracking Period
Jan 18 – Feb 12, 2026
Total Recordings
14 Passes
Orbit Altitude
~408 km

SIGNAL CHARACTERISTICS

  • The ISS cross-band repeater receives on 145.990 MHz (VHF uplink) and transmits on 437.800 MHz (UHF downlink)
  • Sinusoidal wave patterns are characteristic of the FM-modulated carrier signal received from the ISS transponder
  • Doppler shift of approximately ±10 kHz observed as the ISS approaches and recedes, explaining the frequency variation between recordings (437.790 – 437.810 kHz)
  • Signal passes typically last 5–10 minutes during optimal elevation angles above the station's horizon

Dwingeloo Radio Telescope

The Dwingeloo Telescope is a historic 25-meter single-dish radio telescope located in Dwingeloo, Netherlands. Originally built in 1956, it was the largest radio telescope in the world at the time. Today it is operated by the CAMRAS foundation (C.A. Muller Radio Astronomy Station) as a volunteer-run facility.

Dish Diameter
25 meters
Location
Dwingeloo, NL
Established
1956
Operator
CAMRAS
  • Used as a WebSDR node, allowing anyone worldwide to tune into radio signals received by the telescope in real time
  • Capable of receiving signals across a wide range of frequencies, including amateur satellite bands
  • The recordings in this project were captured using the Dwingeloo WebSDR, tuned to the ISS UHF downlink frequency
  • Coordinates: 52°49′19″N, 6°23′36″E

Websites & Online Tools

Key web platforms used during the signal tracking campaign.

WebSDR.org

A Software-Defined Radio receiver connected to the internet, allowing multiple users to listen and tune independently. The Dwingeloo SDR at websdr.ewi.utwente.nl was the primary tool for real-time signal reception and recording.

websdr.org ↗

Morse Code Decoder

Online tool used to decode any Morse Code (CW) signals embedded in the ISS transmissions, helping identify callsigns and telemetry data transmitted through the cross-band repeater.

💻

Software & Tools

Desktop software used for signal analysis, audio processing, and satellite tracking.

Audacity

Open-source audio editing software used for rendering, filtering, and analyzing the recorded WebSDR audio files. Spectrograms helped visualize the sinusoidal signal patterns and Doppler-shifted carrier waves.

audacityteam.org ↗

Gpredict

Real-time satellite tracking and prediction software. Used to determine precise ISS pass times, elevation angles, and AOS/LOS (Acquisition/Loss of Signal) timing for scheduling recording sessions.

gpredict.oz9aec.net ↗
